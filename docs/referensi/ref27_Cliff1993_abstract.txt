Judul  : Dominance statistics: Ordinal analyses to answer ordinal questions.
Penulis: Cliff, Norman
Jurnal : Psychological Bulletin
Tahun  : 1993
DOI    : 10.1037/0033-2909.114.3.494

ABSTRAK / KETERANGAN
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This paper introduces the delta dominance statistic (Cliff's delta) as a non-parametric, ordinal-appropriate measure of effect size. Cliff's delta = P(X > Y) - P(Y > X) where X and Y are two independent samples, ranging from -1 (complete dominance of Y) through 0 (no dominance) to +1 (complete dominance of X). A value of |delta| = 1.000 means every observation from one group exceeds every observation from the other — complete stochastic dominance. This property is used in P5 to characterise the statistical certainty of Sainte-Laguë's advantage over all three baselines in S2.
