ABSTRAK / KETERANGAN
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This paper introduces WF2Q (Worst-Case Fair Weighted Fair Queuing), which provides tighter fairness bounds than WRR and DRR for variable-length packet streams. WF2Q achieves O(1) per-packet overhead while maintaining provably small lag from ideal GPS. The paper analyses scheduling lag in terms of maximum backlog and packet size, providing bounds on within-cycle MAPE that are comparable to the Structural Lock characterisation in P5. Like all prior work in this line, WF2Q analysis assumes static weights.
