ABSTRAK / KETERANGAN
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This is the original 1910 paper by French mathematician André Sainte-Laguë introducing the divisor apportionment method that now bears his name. Sainte-Laguë proved that his method minimises the sum of squared relative errors between actual and proportional seat allocations, using a priority quotient Q(i) = vi/(2*ni+1) where vi is the vote share and ni is the current seat count. This priority quotient structure is directly adopted in the P5 load balancing algorithm, replacing votes/seats with server weights/selection counts.
